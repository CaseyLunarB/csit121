Nigerian Goat Stew

A nice and cozy, melt-in-your-mouth stew for the cold seasons

Recipe

For this recipe, you will need:

  • 1 lb Goat Meat (can be purchased in chopped pieces per pound.)
  • 3 Tomatoes
  • 2 Bell peppers (can be any color among red, green, yellow. I prefer red.)
  • 1 Habenero pepper (can be any color plus you can add more if you prefer
    a spicier dish, or you can skip this ingredient completely if you do not prefer spice.)
  • 1 Onion (can be a yellow or red onion, I find red onions to have more flavor and impact.)
  • 1 teaspoon Pepper
  • 1 ½ Thyme
  • 1 teaspoon Chicken Bouillon Powder
  • ½ teaspoon Curry Powder
  • ½ Salt
  • ¼ cup Palm Oil (can be substituted with olive oil)
  • ½ teaspoon Cayenne Pepper (optional if you do not prefer spice.)
  • 2 cups Beef Stock or Broth

Ok, now that you got your ingredients, let us start the process:

  1. Take 1 lb of goat meat and wash in a bowl of water. Remove any impurities and wash until the water is clear when pouring it out.
  2. Place your salt, pepper, and 1 cup of beef stock or broth in the pot, and place on stovetop on medium heat-low heat until simmering.
  3. Place your goat meat in the pot and cook until the meat is tender (can pull apart with a fork).
  4. While your meat is cooking, wash your vegetables. I reccommend soaking them in warm water with salt for 20 minutes ahead of time to get rid of impurities.
  5. Chop up your tomatoes, bell peppers, habenero pepper, and ½ your onion finely (or you can use a food processor which I heavily reccommend).
  6. Once your meat is done cooking, drain all the water and set aside. You will want to use the pot you boiled your meat in to cook the stew. So place the meat in another container temporarily.
  7. Place the palm oil in the pot, chop up the rest of your onion and fry the onion until translucent.
  8. Once the onions are translucent, place the cooked goat meat in the pot and lightly fry it.
  9. Add in the puree of vegetables from step five. Cook and stir constantly. Add in your thyme, curry powder, chicken bouillon powder, and cayenne pepper.
  10. After cooking for 20 minutes, stirring, and adding seasoning to your taste, the dish is complete. Serve with white rice to balance out the flavor and spice. Enjoy!

About This Recipe

This recipe is my favorite recipe ever, as it has given me great memories. I am not Nigerian myself, but my friend's father cooked this for me, and ever since it has been my favorite African dish. The coziness is brought by the stew texture and flavor that the bouillon brings. It is a perfect dish to eat while watching your favorite movie or televison show.
